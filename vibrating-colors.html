About Color Vibration
Color vibration occurs when two colors with high contrast are placed adjacent to each other, creating an optical illusion where the colors appear to "vibrate" or shimmer. This effect is strongest with complementary colors (opposites on the color wheel) and high saturation. The alternating pattern amplifies the effect, making it more noticeable and intense.
